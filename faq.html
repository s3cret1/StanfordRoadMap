FAQ — Short, Honest Answers

Quick answers to questions most juniors ask first.

Q: Do fly-ins actually help?
A: Yes. They help you learn fit, often include interviews, and admissions offices track attendance. Apply to those you’re genuinely interested in.
Q: Is Matriculate worth it?
A: Many students find Matriculate helpful—it's free and pairs you with undergrads who provide writing feedback and practical deadlines. Experiences vary by advisor, but it's worth applying early.
Q: How many schools should I rank on QuestBridge?
A: Rank only schools you'd happily attend. More schools can increase your match chances, but the Match is binding—do not rank a school you would refuse.
Q: When does the Match Rankings Form open?
A: It commonly opens around October 1—verify yearly dates on the official QuestBridge Dates & Deadlines page before submitting.
Q: What if I don’t match?
A: Not matching is not failure. Apply RD/EA and continue using your essays, test improvements, and advising. Many students still gain elite admits and scholarships.
Q: What should I do first?
A: If you’re a junior: apply to CPS (if eligible), take a diagnostic SAT/ACT, and apply for a Matriculate advisor. Start a single living checklist and update it weekly.
