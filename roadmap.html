Roadmap: Junior → Senior (month-by-month)

QuestBridge deadlines change yearly so please verify official dates beforehand

Junior Year (Big picture)

Goal: strengthen ECs, join CPS & YLS, and begin writing & research early

Jan to Mar

Apply to College Prep Scholars (CPS)

CPS connects you to resources and peers—apply, even if your profile feels incomplete.

  • Complete CPS essays & activities
  • Gather transcript and counselor info
  • Join a supportive community (subreddit/Discord)
Feb — Apr

Plan senior-year schedule & mentors

Avoid avoidable gaps: many colleges expect 3+ years of science or equivalent rigor.

  • Create a 4-year plan with core classes
  • Apply to Matriculate or another free advising program
  • Ask teachers early to be recommenders
Mar — Oct

SAT/ACT — start serious prep

Tests are a controllable lever. Use official practice material and timed tests.

  • Take a diagnostic test
  • Create a weekly practice plan
  • Schedule at least two test dates if possible
Apr — Aug

Fly-ins & summer

Fly-ins are free, high-value experiences—apply to ones that interest you.

  • Apply broadly to fall fly-ins
  • Attend funded summer programs if affordable
  • Draft major essays while fresh in summer
Jun — Nov

Essays & research

Start supplements early. Rank only colleges you’d be excited to attend.

  • Draft personal statement & supplement drafts
  • Get honest feedback; revise multiple times
  • Build a college pros/cons list for ranking
Summer → Senior

Senior-year prep

Finalize last tests, secure recommenders, and prepare documents for Finalist requirements if needed.

  • Confirm test sending & dates
  • Prepare FAFSA/CSS if applicable
  • Keep daily habits small and consistent

Senior year & Match-specific timeline

Common QuestBridge timeline items (double-check official site each year):

  • Match Rankings Form typically opens Oct 1 — confirm dates on QuestBridge site.
  • Finalists are usually notified in mid- to late October (varies by year).
  • If matched: you must follow the partner college's admitted-student steps.
Common Mistake

Ranking schools you wouldn’t attend just to increase chances. The Match is binding—only rank schools you’d happily join.

Daily & weekly habits that work

  • Daily: 25–40 minutes of focused prep (tests or essays) + one short recharge activity.
  • Weekly: at least one full-length practice test or a polished essay revision.
  • Monthly: review timelines, update your templates, and check in with an advisor.
Pro Tip

Write early and revise until it stings. Essays are where voice and reflection win.
