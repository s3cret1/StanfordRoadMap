Mindset & Wellness

Practical emotional care for application season: burnout prevention, impostor scripts, and resilience actions you can use today.

Short rules to live by

  • Small is steady. 25–40 minutes focused work + a break beats all-night marathons.
  • One truth a day. Write one honest sentence about why this matters to you.
  • Sleep helps more than an extra hour of study late at night.

Burnout prevention (practical)

  1. Weekly planning: Block 3–4 focus sessions for app work + 2–3 times for rest/fun.
  2. Pomodoro habit: 25/5 or 50/10: work, then move.
  3. Accountability buddy: Check in weekly with one peer.
Common Mistake

Treating sacrifices as badges—overwork reduces quality. Strategic rest improves output.

Impostor syndrome — scripts & quick moves

When you feel like you don’t belong, use short scripts and concrete evidence to re-center.

Script: “I did the work today. Tomorrow I’ll do one small thing.”
  • List three concrete wins this week (draft completed, practice test, requested LOR).
  • If stuck, message your advisor: “Blocked on X—can we do 20 minutes?”

Rejection resilience

  1. Feel it for 24 hours. Acknowledge the loss.
  2. List two learnings. Turn emotion into action items.
  3. Do one next-step. A revised essay, another application, or a rest day.

Not matching on QuestBridge is not the end—many students still gain top admissions and scholarships through RD/EA.

Family conversations — short scripts

Opener: “I have an important senior-year plan—can I share two things you should know?”

If finances are a concern: “QuestBridge and many summer programs offer funding; here are the official pages.”

Mini relapse plan (if overwhelm spikes)

  1. Stop: Close tabs for 10 minutes and breathe.
  2. List: Write three tiny tasks you can complete in 30 minutes.
  3. Act: Do the smallest task, then take a 20-minute walk.
Your mental health is part of the strategy—protect it.
